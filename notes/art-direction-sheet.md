# Avanti Proposal Art Direction Sheet

## Context
This sheet defines a few viable visual directions for the **Vibeflow proposal for Avanti Restaurant**.

It is based on:
- the current static proposal draft in `working-draft/`
- the current review screenshots
- the Avanti logo asset
- Avanti's visible brand cues: bold red script logo, energetic personality, restaurant/hospitality positioning

## Design goal
The proposal should feel:
- tailored to **Avanti**, not like a generic agency deck
- strong enough for **management discussion and approval**
- warm enough to still feel like a **restaurant growth proposal**
- premium, but not so luxury-generic that Avanti's own identity disappears

---

# Direction A — Avanti Premium Night

## Core idea
A dark premium hospitality presentation with controlled warmth, strong contrast, and executive-level structure.

## Best for
- management presentation
- strategic approval meetings
- positioning Vibeflow as serious, structured, and commercially mature

## Visual character
- near-black / deep charcoal base
- warm amber, bronze, and muted gold highlights
- cream text instead of stark white
- selective serif headlines with modern sans-serif body text
- layered cards, soft glow, dark image frames

## Suggested palette
- Background: `#0A090B`
- Surface: `#121013`
- Surface alt: `#181416`
- Primary text: `#F6F1E8`
- Secondary text: `#CFC3B4`
- Accent gold: `#D7B25F`
- Accent bronze: `#9A6C3C`
- Supporting red: `#A83B32`
- Border: `rgba(255,255,255,0.10)`

## Typography direction
- Headline: high-contrast editorial serif
- Body: clean modern sans
- Eyebrow/meta: compact uppercase sans

## Section style
- strong cover sheet
- premium card grid
- elegant section dividers
- image + insight pairings
- dark proposal sheets with warm highlight lines

## Strengths
- feels high-value and serious
- already close to the current draft, so easiest evolution path
- strong for proposal credibility

## Risks
- can drift into generic luxury-consulting visuals
- Avanti red can become secondary or nearly invisible

## Use if
You want the proposal to say: **"This is a serious growth system for a serious restaurant brand."**

---

# Direction B — Avanti Editorial Dining

## Core idea
A warmer restaurant-first direction with more menu/editorial energy and less dark-boardroom intensity.

## Best for
- making the proposal feel more emotionally tied to food and dining
- a more brand-rich, restaurant-native presentation
- reducing the feeling of a pure consulting deck

## Visual character
- soft cream or warm parchment base
- espresso / dark brown text
- muted Avanti red and olive accents
- generous whitespace
- larger food photography presence
- elegant editorial rhythm, less dashboard feel

## Suggested palette
- Background: `#F6F0E6`
- Surface: `#FFF9F2`
- Surface alt: `#EFE5D6`
- Primary text: `#2E221C`
- Secondary text: `#6A5649`
- Avanti red: `#B2342B`
- Olive accent: `#6D7A43`
- Warm gold: `#B88A4A`
- Border: `rgba(46,34,28,0.10)`

## Typography direction
- Headline: elegant serif with restaurant/editorial feel
- Body: refined sans or readable transitional serif-sans mix
- Numeric or labels: simple utilitarian sans

## Section style
- cleaner paper-like sheets
- stronger story sections
- richer food-image moments
- quote blocks that feel like a magazine profile
- less glow, fewer dark gradients

## Strengths
- more restaurant-specific
- stronger emotional warmth
- easier to tie to brand story, menu, guest experience, and repeat visits

## Risks
- can lose some operational sharpness
- may feel less forceful for high-stakes approval conversations if not structured tightly

## Use if
You want the proposal to say: **"This plan understands Avanti as a restaurant brand, not only as an operations problem."**

---

# Direction C — Avanti Signature Red

## Core idea
A more brand-owned direction that uses Avanti's bold red script identity as the main visual anchor.

## Best for
- visibly making the proposal feel custom to Avanti
- increasing memorability
- bringing stronger local brand recognition into the deck

## Visual character
- charcoal / soft black base or off-white base with red-led accents
- Avanti red as primary accent
- black and cream neutrals
- more assertive callout bars, section labels, and CTA moments
- less reliance on gold-luxury coding

## Suggested palette
- Background: `#0D0D0F`
- Surface: `#17161A`
- Primary text: `#F5F1EA`
- Secondary text: `#C7BFB4`
- Avanti red: `#C4332E`
- Deep red: `#8E231F`
- Soft cream: `#EFE7DC`
- Black: `#111111`
- Border: `rgba(196,51,46,0.22)`

## Typography direction
- Headline: confident serif or strong display sans used with restraint
- Body: practical sans for readability
- Brand moments: use red strategically around headings, section markers, and highlights

## Section style
- stronger Avanti identity on the cover
- red-led highlight cards and chapter dividers
- more contrast between brand moments and analytical sections
- photo treatment can skew warmer and more energetic

## Strengths
- most clearly tied to Avanti's visible brand
- more memorable than a standard premium dark proposal
- better balance between restaurant energy and commercial seriousness than a full gold-luxury deck

## Risks
- too much red can feel aggressive or promotional
- needs disciplined restraint to stay premium

## Use if
You want the proposal to say: **"This is Avanti's growth plan — not a generic restaurant proposal with swapped names."**

# Direction D — Executive Growth Dashboard

## Core idea
A cleaner business-systems direction focused on clarity, metrics, phases, and implementation confidence.

## Best for
- owners/managers who care most about operational logic
- clearer decision flow
- system explanation over brand emotion

## Visual character
- neutral dark or soft-light base
- restrained accents
- card-heavy structure
- cleaner diagrams and tables
- less drama, more clarity

## Suggested palette
- Background: `#0F1115`
- Surface: `#171B22`
- Surface alt: `#1E242D`
- Primary text: `#EEF2F6`
- Secondary text: `#B3BEC8`
- Accent: `#C4332E`
- Secondary accent: `#D6A24F`
- Success: `#2E9F61`
- Border: `rgba(255,255,255,0.10)`

## Typography direction
- Headline: clean, sharp sans or restrained serif-sans blend
- Body: practical sans
- data labels: compact utility style

## Strengths
- most legible for planning and approval
- easiest to map phases, systems, CRM, direct-order logic, automation
- strongest for operational trust

## Risks
- weakest restaurant emotion
- can feel too SaaS / consulting if overused

## Use if
You want the proposal to say: **"Approve this because the system is clear and commercially sound."**

# Recommendation

## Recommended direction
**Avanti Premium Night + Signature Red**

This should be the working direction.

## Why
It gives the best balance of:
- premium presentation quality
- management-facing seriousness
- Avanti-specific visual identity
- restaurant warmth
- proposal memorability

## Recommended visual formula
Keep:
- dark premium sheet structure
- editorial headline moments
- clean consulting-style layout discipline

Change:
- promote **Avanti red** to the main brand accent
- reduce gold from primary accent to secondary support color
- use cream text and warmer neutrals for hospitality softness
- increase restaurant/food/brand cues slightly so it feels less like a luxury strategy template

## Final palette recommendation
- Main background: `#0A090B`
- Main surface: `#121013`
- Main text: `#F6F1E8`
- Secondary text: `#D2C4B5`
- **Primary accent (Avanti red): `#C4332E`**
- Accent dark red: `#8E231F`
- Support gold: `#D7B25F`
- Support bronze: `#9A6C3C`
- Border: `rgba(255,255,255,0.10)`

## Typography recommendation
- Headline: editorial serif for key proposal moments only
- Body: modern sans for all functional reading
- Meta labels: uppercase sans, compact and clean

## Section treatment recommendation
- Hero: dark cinematic with red-led brand accents, not gold-led
- Summary cards: dark neutral cards with thin warm borders and subtle red highlight logic
- Story sections: more restaurant-aware copy blocks and image treatment
- Roadmap/system sections: cleaner, less decorative, more structured
- Quote blocks: warmer, more intimate, less generic strategy-deck language

## What to avoid
- too much gold everywhere
- too many luxury gradients
- too much red everywhere
- over-decorated cards
- making it feel like a fintech pitch or generic premium agency template

---

# Immediate implementation moves for the current draft

1. Replace gold-first emphasis with **red-first emphasis** in hero and key highlight moments.
2. Keep gold only as a supporting highlight, not the main brand signal.
3. Preserve the dark sheet system and editorial headline structure.
4. Tighten some sections so the deck feels more Avanti-specific and less all-purpose strategy presentation.
5. Increase Avanti brand cues in:
   - cover section
   - section separators
   - selected highlight cards
   - image framing

---

# Decision summary
If only one direction is chosen, choose:

> **Avanti Premium Night with Signature Red accents**

That is the strongest path for the Avanti proposal.
